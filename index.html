Kivalina is a town of about 400 inhabitants in the remote North Western part of Alaska. It is about 300 miles from the nearest big town and is accessible only by air or sea. It is in short a pretty remote place.

90% of the village is part of the Inupiaq tribe and 60% is under the age of twenty-five. The town boasts two stores, a post office and a bingo hall, as well as a school and an administrative center.

Kivalina is built on a peninsula that sits in full force of the Pacific ocean. The town is facing the threat of rising sea levels due to climate change, which poses imminent relocation. The pressure for action is growing as the town is losing more and more land to the ocean. KVAK TV is providing an alternative platform for the youth to voice their ideas about the relocation and when it happens, a way to document it.
